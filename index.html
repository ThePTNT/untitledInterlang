The Grammar

Nouns (using nom 'name' as example)
Nominative Singular: nom
Nominative Plural: nomi
Genitive Singular: noma
Genitive Plural: nomai
Prepositional Singular: nome
Prepositional Plural: nomei
Verbs (using audo 'to hear' as example)
Active Infinitive: audo
Active Present: audit
Active Past: auditi
Active Future: audito
Passive Infinitive: sto audum
Passive Present: stit audum
Passive Past: stiti audum
Passive Future: stito audum
Imperative: audam
Prohibitive: ne audam
Adjectives (using bonu 'good' as example)
Adjective: bonu (good)
Opposite Adjective: nebonu (bad)
(yes, that's it!)

Phonology
Plosives: p b t d k g
Nasals: m n
Fricatives: f v s z š /ʃ/ ž /ʒ/ h
Affricates: č /tʃ/ dž /dʒ/
Approximants: w l i /j/
Extra Characters: c /k/ /s/

When 'c' is before a, o, u, before a consonant, or word finally, /k/

When 'c' is before e or i, /s/

Adjective Intensifiers (using contentu 'happy' as example)
Normal: contentu (happy)
Comparative: contenteru (happier)
Superlative: contentestu (happiest)
Converting Adjectives to Nouns (using contentu 'happy' as example)
Adjective: contentu (happy) [person contentu = happy person]
Noun: le content / content (the happy one / a happy one) [le content audit eo = the happy one hears that]
Converting Nouns to Verbs (using nom 'name' as example)
Noun: nom (name) [vo habit nom = you have a name]
Verb: nomo (to be named) [vo nomit ao = you are named something]
Converting Adjectives to Adverbs (using bonu 'good' as example)
Noun: bonu (good) [dom bonu = good house]
Verb: bonur (well) [io bonur duit džio = I do/make it well]
Questions
Normal: Vo habit nom. (You have a name)
Interrogative: Sa vo habit nom? (Do you have a name)

Numbers are constructed by going in order from lowest digit to highest, and separating numbers using dashes (-) and the word for and, 'i'.
Multiples of ten, hundred, and thousand are constructed by adding the number of tens/hundreds/thousands and then the word for the digit. Examples:
Eleven = un-i-desen (one-and-ten)
Seventy-four = čotron-i-septemdesen (four-and-seventen)
Nine hundred and fifty-six = šeist-i-fiatdesen-i-nevcent (six-and-fiveten-and-ninehundred)
Ordinal Numbers
To construct an ordinal number, take the number and add the suffix -alu to the end. Examples:
Eleventh = un-i-desenalu
Seventy-fourth = čotron-i-septemdesenalu
Nine hundred and fifty-sixth = šeist-i-fiatdesen-i-nevcentalu
This can also convert to adjectives and adverbs as follows:
Adjective: unalu -> unal (l'unal = the first one)
Adverb: unalu -> unalur (duam džio unalur = do it first)
Negation
Negation is pretty simple, just add the word 'ne' meaning no before the verb. Examples: I don't understand. = io ne comprehendit. I am not 30 years old. = io ne habit trindesen iari. (lit. I don't have 30 years) No! = ne!
Correlatives
These work like in Esperanto, except the endings can be irregular at some times to separate diphthongs like ee and aa.
